Iceland

Iceland is one of top 10 destinations to travel this year

About Iceland

Iceland is a Nordic island nation. Iceland is defined by its dramatic landscape with volcanoes, geysers, hot springs and lava fields. Massive glaciers are protected in Vatnajökull and Snæfellsjökull national parks. Most of the population lives in the capital, Reykjavik, which runs on geothermal power and is home to the National and Saga museums, tracing Iceland’s Viking history.

When to visit

Iceland has a sub-polar climate. The peak travel period is during summer (Jun–Aug), with mild weather and nearly 24 hours of daylight. Thjodhatid (Aug, Heimaey Island) is an outdoor music festival with bonfires and fireworks. The Iceland Airwaves music festival (Nov, Reykjavik) features world-famous artists and Icelandic bands. Winter (Dec–Feb) is cold, dark and rainy, with strong wind. The Winter Lights Festival (Feb, Reykjavik) has illuminated landmarks and cultural events.

Travelers typically stay 4–8 days.
