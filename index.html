No trip to Ireland is complete without a visit to County Galway on the west coast of the country.

Home to over 250,000 people and named the European Capital of Culture for 2020, this vibrant region offers something for everyone â€“ rugged countryside, beautiful beaches, thriving city culture, historical castles, amazing food and plenty of Guinness.

Galway County was first inhabited over 7,000 years ago and originally comprised of several kingdoms and territories that predate the formation of the county.

Visitors flock to Galway year-round, and is particularly busy around key events like the Galway International Arts Festival, the Galway Races and the Galway Oyster Festival.

Plan your trip for the spring or summer when your chances at a dry day are slightly better than the rest of the year (rainy days are a common occurrence in the west of Ireland).
